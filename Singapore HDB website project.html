The Housing & Development Board (HDB) is Singapore's public housing authority. They are the main authority who plan and develop Singapore's housing estates to create quality living environments for every singaporean. They provide various commercial, recreational, and social amenities in Singapores towns as well.

Established during the 1960’s housing crisis, they were tasked with replacing and developing the various slums and squater settlements in singapore with high quality public housing. Many residents had resisted the development due to fears of displacement, but after a fire had gone through their neighbourhoods they had accepted these developments.

Singapore's public housing has housed an entire nation – In 2018, the rate of home ownership in Singapore was approximately 91 percent of the population. HDB flats spell home for 80% of Singapore's resident population, of which about 90% own their home.

For over 50 years, They have successfully provided quality and affordable public housing for generations of Singaporeans. This has been shown with its true accomplishment being 90% flat, neighborhood, and estate satisfaction. They have proven that housing can be high quality, while at the same time, remaining affordable for everyone.
